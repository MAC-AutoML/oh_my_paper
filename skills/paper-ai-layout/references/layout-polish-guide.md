# Layout and language polish guide

Use this after story, claims, and evidence are stable. Do not let polishing hide unsupported science.

## Layout checks

- Venue/template/page limit known or explicitly unknown.
- Title/abstract/first figure visible and readable.
- Figures/tables placed near first reference when possible.
- Appendix contains support, not essential first-order evidence.
- References, anonymity, supplement, and checklist requirements satisfied.
- PDF compiles cleanly; no overfull boxes or broken references if LaTeX is available.

## Language polish modes

- Clarify logic without changing claim strength.
- Shorten while preserving information.
- Expand implicit reasoning when the reader needs support.
- Translate Chinese/English academic text while preserving LaTeX/math/citations.
- Remove AI-like verbosity and generic phrases.

## Sentence-level checks

- Long sentences with multiple claims.
- Passive voice that hides agents when agents matter.
- Weak words: very, clearly, significant without evidence.
- Article/preposition errors in English.
- Inconsistent terms or notation.

## Common failures

- Polishing before fixing the argument.
- Compressing away caveats needed for correctness.
- Changing technical meaning during translation.
- Making claims sound stronger than evidence permits.

## Material-derived case card

Source excerpt (rights-cleared writing template):

> 论文收获好review的关键：把论文做得漂亮、美观，让人第一印象觉得这篇论文很高级。怎么让论文第一眼看起来很漂亮、高级：1. 好看的teaser figure、pipeline figure。2. 好看的表格和结果图。3. 整齐的排版。

Imitation checklist:

- Before final polish, inspect page 1 as an image/PDF, not just source text.
- Check teaser/pipeline visual alignment, table neatness, result plot consistency, whitespace, and caption density.
- Treat layout as reviewer trust-building, not decoration.

Bad final pass:

> Grammar fixed; ready.

Good final pass:

> Page 1 visually communicates problem and method; teaser labels readable; abstract claim C2 caveated; Table 1 alignment fixed; no overfull boxes; title does not overclaim.
